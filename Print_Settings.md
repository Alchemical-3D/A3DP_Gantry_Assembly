# Print Settings for A3DP Gantry

The structural printed parts of the A3DP Gantry must be printed in rigid, temperature-resistant materials. We strongly recommend **ABS, ASA, or PC (Polycarbonate)**. Standard PLA or PETG will deform under typical enclosure temperatures or over time from belt tensioning.

## Slicer Recommendations (0.4mm Nozzle)

Below are the suggested values for a reliable, functional print to balance weight, strength, and print duration.

| Parameter | Recommended Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Layer Height** | 0.2mm |
| **Extrusion Width** | 0.4mm - 0.45mm |
| **Wall Perimeters** | 4 (minimum) |
| **Top / Bottom Solid Layers** | 5 Top / 5 Bottom |
| **Infill Type** | Grid, Gyroid, or Rectilinear |
| **Infill Percentage** | 40% |
| **Supports** | None required. |

### Pro-Tips
- **Shrinkage Compensation:** If using ABS/ASA, remember to ensure your filament flow and scaling are dialed in so bearings and pins fit snugly without cracking the plastic. Overtight components mean your shrinkage isn't calibrated. You can print out a common 20mm calibration cube to adjust your filament shrinkage scale as required.
- **Cooling:** Depending on your current printer, use minimal part cooling to ensure excellent layer adhesion for maximum structural integrity.

Once you have your parts printed, proceed to the [Assembly Guide](Assembly_Guide.md) and check your [Bill of Materials](BOM.md) for required hardware.
